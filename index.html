▼ Exhibitions & Film

  • Seeing Like the U.S.-Mexico Border. June 2020. Los Angeles Contemporary Archive. The exhibition features archival projects that document digital life, humour and social critique in the U.S.-Mexico border. It includes contributions in counter-mapping state abandonment in Arizona desert zones, an archival platform of visual reportage from border entry ports, and an archive of the “paper trail” of migration. Curated by Jorge Rodríguez Solórzano, with the support of SOAS Student Fund Award. Collaboration with LACA & Tara Plath.
  • Here to Stay. November 2019. London International Documentary Film Festival. The documentary tells the story of Altab Ali Park and anti-racist activism following the stabbing of Altab Ali, a Sylheti migrant killed in Whitechapel in 1978. Threading together interviews with activists from the Bengali diaspora and archival footage, the documentary traces the community’s decades-long effort to create a public space of memory and collective action, and revisits the legacies of the anti-racist Bengali movement in East London.

About

Jorge Alejandro Rodríguez Solórzano is a writer and researcher from Guadalajara, Jalisco. His work investigates displacement, migration and diaspora, as well as their generative and political dimensions, through auto-fictive writing and collaborative, multi-media work. 

Jorge’s creative writing and literary translations have been published or are forthcoming in Oral.Pub, rivulet press, Khabar Keslan, Lifta Volumes: Futures of Palestine, and Angelaki Journal for the the Theoretical Humanities. His recent film and curatorial work has been shown or is forthcoming at the London International Migration Film Festival and the Los Angeles Contemporary Archive. 

Jorge has been a fellow at the London in Motion Documentary Workshop and at the Los Angeles Review of Books/ University of Southern California Publishing Workshop. He holds an MA degree with distinction in Migration and Diaspora Studies from SOAS, University of London, and a BA in French Literature and Language from Reed College.

He resides in the ever-expanding Mexico-U.S. border.
